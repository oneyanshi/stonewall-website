WHAT HAPPENED?

In the early morning hours of June 28, 1969, police raided the Stonewall Inn, a popular gay bar in the Greenwich Village section of New York City. In an era where homosexuality was seen as a mental illness and was considered illegal in all the states but Illinois, police raids on gay bars and clubs were not out of the norm. Most raids were settled peacefully.

But this raid was different.

People fought back the police in narrow streets, resulting in riots that lasted for nearly a week. Patrons resisted arrest and police armed themselves with riot gear and beating sticks. Mainstream newspapers like the New York Daily News wrote articles that ridiculed LGBT+ community. However, Stonewall encouraged other publications to rise, "to come out of the closet," and defend the community, giving them a call to action.
