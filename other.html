OTHER PUBLICATIONS

Other publications of the time were newspapers like Gay Sunshine, Gay Times, and Killer Dyke voiced radical viewpoints. Gay Sunshine outright targetted heterosexuals and much of their illustrations drew inspiration from publications from The Black Panthers. They were a defiant voice that sought change through radical means. Gay Times endorsed violence through its logo and was a one-sheet newsletter that the editor, John O'Brien, taped to trees and utility poles (Streitmatter, 246). Finally, Killer Dyke, a miliant feminist lesbian focused tabloid centered in Chicago advocated for violence as well through writing and provoking images.

A publication that is argurably the oldest LGBT+ magazine, "The Advocate," founded in 1967, was created prior to The Stonewall and is ongoing today. They reported on the Stonewall riots and championed the rights of LGBT+ community. Much like GAY, "The Advocate" promoted non-violence and sought education of American society versus dismantling the American society.
